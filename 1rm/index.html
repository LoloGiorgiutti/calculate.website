About 1RM and training zones

1RM (One Repetition Maximum) is the maximum weight you can lift for a single repetition with correct form. It's the universal standard for measuring strength in powerlifting and strength sports.

This calculator averages three validated formulas — Epley (w × (1 + r/30)), Brzycki (w × 36/(37−r)) and Lander (100w / (101.3 − 2.67123×r)) — for the best estimate. All formulas are most accurate for 2–10 reps.

Use the training zone table to set loads for your program: strength (85–100%), hypertrophy (65–80%), endurance (<65%).

Frequently asked questions
What is 1RM?
1RM (One Repetition Maximum) is the maximum weight you can lift for a single repetition with correct form. It is the standard measure of strength in exercises like squat, bench press and deadlift.
Which 1RM formula is most accurate?
The Epley and Brzycki formulas are the most widely used and validated. They are most accurate for sets of 2–10 reps. For higher rep counts the estimates become less reliable. Averaging multiple formulas (as this calculator does) reduces error.
Should I test my 1RM directly?
Direct 1RM testing carries injury risk, especially for beginners. Using a submaximal test — e.g., how many reps you can do with a given weight — and applying a formula is safer and practical for most people.
How often should I re-test 1RM?
Most athletes re-test every 4–8 weeks after a training block. Re-testing helps track progress and adjust training loads to match your current strength level.
