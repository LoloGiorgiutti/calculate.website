What is BMI and how does it work?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is the most widely used international indicator to assess whether a person's weight is appropriate relative to their height. It was developed in the 19th century by mathematician Lambert Adolphe Quetelet and adopted by the World Health Organization (WHO) as the standard reference measure.

The formula is simple: BMI = weight in kg ÷ height² in meters (or BMI = weight in lb × 703 ÷ height² in inches). The result is interpreted according to WHO ranges: underweight (below 18.5), normal weight (18.5–24.9), overweight (25–29.9) and obese (30 or more).

Important: BMI is a population-level indicator, not a diagnosis. It does not distinguish between muscle mass and body fat, and may not be representative for athletes, older adults or people with body compositions very different from the average. Always consult a health professional for a complete assessment.

Frequently asked questions
What is BMI?
BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure that relates a person's weight and height. It is the most widely used indicator globally to assess whether weight is in a healthy range according to WHO criteria.
How is BMI calculated?
The formula is: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m). For example, a person weighing 70 kg and 1.75 m tall has a BMI of 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9, which is in the normal weight range.
What are the BMI ranges according to WHO?
Underweight: below 18.5  ·  Normal weight: 18.5 to 24.9  ·  Overweight: 25 to 29.9  ·  Obese class I: 30 to 34.9  ·  Obese class II: 35 to 39.9  ·  Obese class III: 40 or more.
Is BMI enough to know if I'm healthy?
BMI is a useful starting point but has important limitations: it does not distinguish between muscle and fat. A muscular person may have an "overweight" BMI without actually being overweight. For a complete assessment, consult a health professional.
What is my ideal weight range?
The "healthy" BMI range (18.5–24.9) translates to: minimum weight = 18.5 × height² and maximum weight = 24.9 × height². For 5'7" (170 cm): between 118 lb and 159 lb (53.5 kg – 72 kg). The actual "ideal weight" also depends on body composition and age.
