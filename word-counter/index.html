Frequently asked questions
How does the word counter count words?
Words are counted by splitting the text on whitespace and filtering out empty tokens. Hyphenated words like well-known count as one word. Numbers and symbols surrounded by spaces count as individual tokens.
What reading speed does the calculator use?
The calculator assumes an average adult reading speed of 238 words per minute, the median found in large-scale studies. Speaking time uses 130 wpm, typical for a clear presentation pace.
Does the character count include spaces?
Both counts are shown: Characters includes every character including spaces; Chars (no spaces) strips all whitespace before counting.
How are sentences counted?
Sentences are detected by counting sentence-ending punctuation: periods, exclamation marks, and question marks. If no punctuation is found the text is counted as one sentence.
Is there a character limit?
There is no hard limit — the tool works with texts of any length. Very long texts may take a fraction of a second longer to process the word frequency analysis.
