How is the monthly payment calculated?
This calculator uses the fixed-rate (equal installment) method — also known as French amortization. Each monthly payment is the same amount. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal. This is the most common method for personal loans and mortgages.
Frequently asked questions
How is the monthly loan payment calculated?
The monthly payment uses the standard amortization formula: M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1], where P is the principal, r is the monthly rate (APR ÷ 12), and n is the number of months.
What is APR vs EAR?
APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the stated rate used to compute payments. EAR (Effective Annual Rate) accounts for monthly compounding and reflects the true annual cost — always slightly higher than APR.
Should I pay off my loan early?
Paying extra principal early in the loan typically reduces total interest significantly, since early payments are mostly interest. Always check for prepayment penalties before making extra payments.
What is an amortization schedule?
An amortization schedule is a month-by-month table showing each payment split into principal and interest portions, plus the remaining balance. Click the button above to view the full schedule for your loan.
