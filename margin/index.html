How it works
Margin = Profit ÷ Revenue × 100. Calculated on what you charge.
Markup = Profit ÷ Cost × 100. Calculated on what you pay. Always higher than margin.
Example: you buy at $100 and sell at $150 → Margin = 33.3% · Markup = 50% · Profit = $50
Frequently asked questions
What is profit margin?
Profit margin is the percentage of revenue that is profit: Margin = (Price − Cost) ÷ Price × 100. A 33% margin means $33 of every $100 in revenue is profit.
What is the difference between margin and markup?
Margin is calculated on the selling price. Markup is calculated on the cost. Buy at $100, sell at $150: Margin = 33.3%, Markup = 50%. Markup is always higher.
What is a good profit margin?
It depends on the industry. Retail typically targets 30–50% gross margin. Software and services can be 60–80%+. Net profit margins after all expenses are typically 5–20%.
How do I calculate selling price from cost and margin?
Price = Cost ÷ (1 − Margin%). For a $100 cost with 30% margin: Price = $100 ÷ 0.70 = $142.86. Note: adding 30% markup to $100 gives only $130 — a different result!
