Frequently asked questions
How is exact age calculated?
Your exact age is calculated by comparing your date of birth with today's date, counting complete years elapsed. The remaining months and days are the period between your last birthday and today.
How many days old am I?
The total number of days lived is calculated from your birth date to today, including leap years. A 30-year-old has lived approximately 10,950 days.
What is my generation?
Generations are defined by birth year: Baby Boomers (1946–1964), Generation X (1965–1980), Millennials (1981–1996), Generation Z (1997–2012), and Generation Alpha (2013–present).
How do leap years affect age calculation?
Leap years have 366 days instead of 365, which affects the total day count. The calculator accounts for leap years automatically when computing days and hours.
