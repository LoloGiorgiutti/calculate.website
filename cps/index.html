Frequently asked questions
What is a good CPS score?
The average person clicks at about 6–8 CPS. Experienced gamers typically reach 10–14 CPS. Scores above 14 CPS are exceptional, and anything above 16 CPS is considered elite level. World records exceed 25 CPS using butterfly clicking techniques.
What clicking techniques improve CPS?
Regular clicking: standard single-finger clicking, good for 6–10 CPS. Jitter clicking: tensing your arm and hand to vibrate your finger, reaching 10–14 CPS. Butterfly clicking: alternating two fingers on one button, can exceed 16 CPS. Drag clicking: sliding your finger to register multiple clicks in one stroke — not suitable for this test.
Why does my score drop in longer tests?
Hand fatigue is the main factor. Muscles and tendons tire quickly at maximum clicking speed. Professional gamers train hand endurance alongside clicking technique. Try stretching your hands before a long test.
Is CPS important for gaming?
In some games (PvP combat in Minecraft, for example) CPS directly affects performance. In most modern FPS games, raw click speed matters less than aim precision and reaction time. A consistent 7–10 CPS is sufficient for most games.
